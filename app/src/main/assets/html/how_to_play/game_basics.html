Quantro
Game Basics


Gameplay

Quantro is a falling-block puzzle game. The object is to keep the blocks from stacking to the top of the screen by completely filling rows, which are automatically cleared away.

Pieces fall one at a time from the top of the screen. Most pieces are composed of four blocks - Retro games use tetrominoes (made of four squares), while Quantro games also include tetracubes (four cubes). Custom games can additional piece types.

You can move, turn, and drop falling pieces until they lock in place after landing. Custom game modes may also allow flips.

If a piece completes a full row when it locks, that row will be cleared to make room for more blocks.

The game is over when blocks stack to the top of the game field and there is no room for another piece to enter.

Screen Layout

The center of the screen shows the game field, where the currently falling piece is controlled. The left edge includes a preview of the next piece to enter and, in Versus mode, a thumbnail of your opponent's game. The right edge shows the piece currently held in reserve (or ready for attack) and an abbreviated description of your current level and score. Both edges provide reminders of the location of any on-screen controls.

Scoring Points

Points are earned primarily by clearing rows (dropping a piece will also earn a small number of points). The more rows cleared at once or in a cascade resulting from a single piece, the more points will be earned. Additionally, more points can be earned at higher game levels.

Points earned are multiplied against your current multiplier before being added to your total. Your multiplier will begin at ×1.0 and can be increased by certain in-game actions. If you go long enough without clearing rows or increasing your multiplier, it will gradually drop back to ×1.0.

In Retro and Quantro games, your multiplier increases when:

  • Two or more rows are cleared with a gap (a row that isn't cleared) between them.
  • A row is cleared by an immobile piece: a piece that locked where it couldn't move in any direction (even up). Use turns to lock pieces this way.

In Quantro games, your multiplier will also increase when:

  • A 3D piece clears completely after landing.
  • A fused piece locks, activates, and causes a row clear.
  • A flash piece unlocks blocks, causing a row clear.
  • At least one row in both the foreground and the background is cleared by a links piece.

Raising and maintaining your score multiplier is the key to high scoring games.
