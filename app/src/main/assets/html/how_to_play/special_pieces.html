Quantro Special Pieces

Quantro games use four basic types of special pieces. These pieces appear randomly in reserve and can be used at any time to replace the currently falling piece.

The normal tetrominoes and tetracubes in Quantro will stack and clear separately in the foreground and background. Special pieces, by contrast, will often interact with both the foreground and background in unique ways. Because of this, special pieces can be dangerous if you aren't careful, but when used skillfully can dramatically improve your chances in a game.

Quantro blocks.

Flash Pieces

The orange Flash piece is a single block in either the foreground or background (with a red- or blue-tinted border, respectively). Instead of rotating, this piece will shift between the foreground and background when turned

When it lands, a Flash piece will disappear instead of locking in place. However, every block directly above the Flash piece - in both the foreground and background - will unlock from their neighbors and fall, filling gaps and possibly setting up row clears.

Flash pieces should be landed as low in the field as possible, directly under a large column of blocks. The Flash piece's small size and its ability to shift between foreground and background allow it to be maneuvered into very tight spaces deep within a stack of blocks.

3D Tetrominoes

Green 3D tetrominoes extend into both the foreground and the background; they stack with both red and blue blocks. However, a 3D block will be removed if either the foreground or background row clears. 3D tetrominoes provide a reward if they are completely cleared immediately after landing, before the next piece can appear: either an entire column of blocks will unlock and fall to fill gaps, or new blocks will appear and fall into available valleys.

3D tetrominoes should be used only when you're sure they can fill enough rows to be completely removed - a 3D block sitting in the field can be very difficult to maneuver around.
Links

Links appear in unconnected groups of four, shaped like a tetromino. A Link includes a foreground and a background component, linked together. Each Link will fall independently into cracks, without locking to blocks to the left or right, but the foreground and background components of a single Link will not separate until one has been cleared away.

Links can be useful for filling awkward gaps, but require careful consideration of both the foreground and background to be used effectively.
Fused Tetracube

The foreground and background blocks in a normal tetracube separate upon landing; the grey-tinted block in a Fused tetracube, on the other hand, connects the foreground and background together. If carelessly placed, a Fused tetracube can permanently join foreground and background blocks into a single stack.

Through careful positioning, a Fused block can be "activated." An activated block changes the way its row is cleared - instead of foreground and background clearing independently, all blocks in the row will be cleared together. In other words, when a Fused block is activated, the row will be cleared as if the foreground and background have been flattened together.

A Fused tetracube will activate upon locking, provided all of its foreground blocks have locked in front of existing background blocks (and not empty space), and its background blocks behind existing foreground blocks. A Fused block remains activated until it causes a clear.

An activated Fused block is about to clear this row: although the foreground and background have gaps, the row would be full if both were flattened together.

Special Versus Pieces

Certain mulitplayer game modes provide special block types apart from those found in most game types.
3D Blocks

Green 3D blocks can be used to attack your opponent in Bitter Pill mode. When used, they fall into your opponent's game field, landing on existing blocks. Like a 3D tetromino, the 3D blocks extend into both the foreground and the background, and can be removed if either the foreground or background row clears. Clearing a 3D block will allow you to send it back to your opponent.

Push Blocks

Push blocks appear in Gravity mode, which otherwise uses Retro blocks. Blue, downward-pointing push blocks will push the bottom row in your game field into your opponent's game, while orange, upward-pointing push blocks do the reverse.

Push blocks can be cleared in the normal way, but clearing one has a unique effect. Clearing one of your own downward-pointing push blocks will result in a "penalty" garbage row; clearing one of your opponent's upward-pointing push blocks will return the pushed row from your game field back to your opponent's.
