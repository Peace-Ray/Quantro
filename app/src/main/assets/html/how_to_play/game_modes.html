Quantro
Game Types


Games in Quantro come in two basic "styles:" Retro, which uses familiar pieces and game ideas, and Quantro, which builds on the familiar formula using new pieces and gameplay mechanics. Both styles are available in a variety of game modes.

Retro Games

Retro games use seven different tetrominoes (shapes made of four squares) that fall in a random order, stacking in the two-dimensional field.

Custom Retro games can also include trominoes (three squares) and pentominoes (five squares).

When a reserve piece is used, it will swap positions with the currently falling piece. This lets you hold a piece in reserve until you find the perfect opportunity to use it.

Quantro Games

Quantro games use seven tetrominoes combined with seven different tetracubes (shapes made of four cubes). Tetrominoes and tetracubes stack in a three-dimensional field.

Blocks in the foreground are semi-transparent, and tinted red. Blocks in the background are tinted blue. Tetrominoes enter in either the foreground or the background, and are colored accordingly; tetracubes include both foreground and background blocks.

The reserve holds random special pieces which can provide a significant advantage if used skillfully. When used, these pieces replace the currently falling piece, which will reappear as the next piece to enter.

Foreground and background blocks stack - and clear - independently. Certain special pieces will interact with blocks in the foreground and background at the same time.

Game Modes

Quantro includes many different game modes: Endurance, Progression, Flood, Versus, Flood Versus, and several advanced and unique versus game modes. Most game modes can be played using Quantro or Retro pieces.

Single Player

In Endurance mode, pieces enter from the top one-by-one until the game ends. After a certain number of rows are cleared, the game will smoothly transition to the next level without any interruption in gameplay. At higher levels pieces fall faster but higher scores are possible. Endurance games can be optionally started with garbage - field rows partially filled with blocks - for an added challenge. An Endurance game ends when the field fills with blocks. Endurance mode is for 1 player only.

Progression mode allows players to take a break between levels, and even repeat the most recent level as many times as they like before moving on. Because pieces fall faster at higher levels, you may want to replay a level several times until you end in an advantageous position. Optionally, new garbage rows can be added between levels. In a Progression game you can restart the current level if the screen fills with blocks. Progression mode is for 1 player only.

Flood mode presents players with the constant threat of blocks rising from below the game field. These rows are added in real-time, requiring fast reaction by players to stay ahead of the rising tide. Higher levels bring faster speeds, for both falling pieces and rising rows. Flood games can be optionally started with garbage rows, and the game ends when the field fills with blocks. Flood mode is for 1 player only.

Multiplayer

Versus mode provides a head-to-head competition between players. Like Endurance mode there are no breaks between levels. When you clear 2 or more rows at once, garbage rows will be sent to your opponent, pushing all the blocks in their game field up after their next piece lands. A Versus game ends when either your or your opponent's field fills with blocks - whichever player still has room for new pieces is the winner. Versus mode is for 2 players only.

Flood Versus mode allows competitive gameplay with the slowly-rising blocks of Flood mode. When you clear 2 or more rows at once, your opponent's rising flood of blocks will temporarily accelerate. Keep clearing multiple rows to keep the pressure on your opponent. A Flood Versus game ends when either your or your opponent's field fills with blocks. Flood Versus mode is for 2 players only.

Advanced Multiplayer

Bitter Pill is an advanced multiplayer game mode using Quantro blocks. Unlike most Quantro game types, no reserve is available. Instead, the reserve button is replaced by an attack button, which allows you to send up to four 3D blocks into your opponent's game. These blocks can be difficult to work around, interfering with your opponent's strategy and giving you a competitive advantage. Earn more 3D blocks by clearing rows, especially rows that contain 3D blocks your opponent has sent into your own game field. Bitter Pill is for 2 players only.

Gravity is an advanced multiplayer game mode using Retro blocks. Unlike most Retro game types, you cannot place game pieces in reserve; instead, push blocks are available to replace your current piece. Push blocks can be earned by clearing enough block rows, and up to four can be held in reserve at once. Use push blocks to send rows from your screen to your opponent's, but be careful setting up row clears — clear away your own push blocks and you'll receive a penalty row. Gravity is for 2 players only.
